The map is your helper


Hello, friends! Packing a backpack in anticipation of new wanderings? Did you forget the cards?


A paper card does not need the Internet, does not waste battery power, and in general gives you the opportunity to take a break from gadgets and feel like a pioneer from the past. It is edited with a simple pencil, decorated with flags and proudly flies on the wall. Unites in the campaign: you can bend over a huge map with the whole group - not like over a smartphone. Unfortunately, the disadvantages are obvious. The cards get wet, crumple and eventually become irrelevant.


Of course, in the modern world we use mobile applications every day. There are a lot of mobile apps with maps: Maps me, Fatmap, Spyglass, Locus map and others.


Mobile applications are most convenient to use as a navigator for pedestrians and motorists. In some cities, public transport displays. Contains voice instructions. You can enable night mode and 3D format. Maps of many foreign cities have been drawn.


Applications allow you to share GPS coordinates, which means that you can report yourself to civilization if you get lost on the route. Simple designations of mobile maps do not require knowledge of topography, and descriptions of objects are convenient to use for path planning. The good old paper map will not be out of use soon. A large format is more convenient to consider, and it will also help out if the phone sits down, breaks or gets lost, especially on complex, autonomous routes.


The ideal preparation for a hike is paper maps and several applications. Start getting to know them at home while you have access to the Internet and electricity.
