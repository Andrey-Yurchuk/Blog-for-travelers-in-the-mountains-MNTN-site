Go to the mountains

What need to take with you to the mountains?

It's simple! Here are some rules for you

Rule #1. The warmer, the better

Going to the mountains, even in the height of summer, be sure to take warm clothes with you. It's not just about a jacket or a sweater. You will need thermal underwear, a hat, and gloves.

Rule #2. Layering

We repeat: traveling through the mountains, you will most likely have to cross several climatic zones at once, which means that the temperature difference is guaranteed. Therefore do not be lazy to wear several things at once. Let you be wearing a T–shirt, a warm jacket, and a down vest. You can always throw off the top "layer" or, conversely, quickly insulate yourself as needed. There should also be spare shoes on hand. In the flat part, you may need light fabric sneakers, but above, in the mountains, you can not do without special shoes that fit the leg tightly.

Rule #3. Maximum closeness

Even if the day is sunny and warm, wear only closed clothes. Shorts, T-shirts and flip–flops are not suitable for mountain hiking. The body should be closed as much as possible. This way you can avoid sunburn – it is much easier to get them in the mountains than on the flat part.

Rule #4. Bet on synthetics

Don’t be afraid to choose clothes made of high-tech synthetic fabrics for your trekking wardrobe. The density and strength of things should be high, and the fabric should be easy to dry and let in air. In addition, things made of synthetic fabrics are much lighter than natural ones, such as wool.

Rule #5. Bright colors

Buy clothes of bright colors for hiking in the mountains. This will not only provide you with a good mood during the trip, but it will also be very practical. In bright–colored clothes, you will be seen even at a long distance – in case you get lost. But a bright color should be chosen wisely. Orange shades are considered optimal at any time of the year: they always stand out against the background of the rest of the landscape.

Below you can read what things we recommend to wear

What to wear to the mountains in summer? Trekking shoes – lightweight model, breathing. Choosing between a low and a high top, you need to assess the complexity of the route, the intensity of effort and the nature of the mining activity.
Trekking pants are preferably modular pants that turn into shorts in a matter of seconds.
A lightweight T–shirt that breathes.
Waterproof jacket.
Hat / bandana / shawl.
Lightweight breathable jacket.
Sunglasses.
What to wear to the mountains in winter and autumn? Autumn and winter in the mountains are more demanding. During this time of year, you should choose trekking shoes with a high top and a waterproof membrane. It should be a insulated model. In winter and autumn, do not forget about thermal clothing that maintains body heat, but also breathes at the same time. You should choose a high–quality fleece and a membrane jacket. Trousers should be insulated.
Tourist vests are perfect as an additional layer that does not restrict movement. Also pay attention to additional pockets for small items. You should have a warm hat and gloves. Winter clothing in the mountains should keep you warm and protect you from the wind. As in summer, you should have sports sunglasses with you. The sun’s rays reflecting off the snow are as unpleasant as the hot summer sun.
What to wear to the mountains in spring? In spring, you need to remember that the old snow cover settles in the mountains – especially in the higher parts of the mountains. Having reached the top, you can be overtaken by a strong wind and the temperature is much lower. Therefore, when choosing clothes for the mountains in the spring, you should adhere to the same criteria as when choosing winter clothes. Remember the rule of 3 layers, which we have described in more detail here.
Spring is a time of increased precipitation, so a high–quality rain jacket and a membrane backpack cover are absolutely necessary. After all, it is in the backpack that everything is needed in the mountains, so we must protect its contents from moisture.
